## 🎮 About the Project 🎮
Little Lantern Stall is a 2D cozy management game that I developed as my senior capstone project at California State University, Dominguez Hills. Players run a small Japanese yakitori stall where they serve customers, earn coins, unlock upgrades, and expand their business. Throughout the game, players also encounter cybersecurity-related events that encourage them to recognize scams, suspicious behavior, and social engineering attempts. Instead of teaching cybersecurity through long explanations or quizzes, I wanted players to learn naturally through gameplay and decision-making.

## 💡 Why I Built This 💡
I've always enjoyed cozy games like Stardew Valley, Unpacking, A Little to the Left, and Minecraft because they create relaxing experiences while giving players a satisfying sense of progression. As a Computer Technology student specializing in Homeland Security, I also became interested in cybersecurity awareness and how many security incidents happen because of human decisions rather than technical failures. I wanted to combine these two interests into a project that felt approachable and enjoyable instead of overwhelming. Little Lantern Stall became my way of exploring whether cybersecurity concepts could be introduced through a cozy management game rather than traditional educational methods.

## ✨ Features ✨
- Manage a cozy yakitori stall
- Serve customers and earn coins
- Upgrade your stall to attract more customers
- Respond to cybersecurity events
- Identify phishing-style scam popups
- Interact with suspicious NPCs
- Earn buffs for good security decisions
- Receive temporary debuffs after unsafe choices

## 🔐 Cybersecurity Concepts 🔐
Little Lantern Stall introduces cybersecurity awareness through gameplay rather than direct instruction.

Players learn by interacting with:

- Phishing-style scam popups
- Suspicious NPC encounters
- Security upgrade systems
- Gameplay rewards for safe decisions
- Gameplay penalties for risky decisions

The goal is to encourage players to slow down, recognize suspicious situations, and think before acting.
